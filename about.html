Tajweed Colors Roles:

  1. Hamzah Wasl
    Alif is located at the beginning of a word without any punctuation at all. Hamzah washl is only said at the beginning of a word, but not read when it is in the middle of a sentence.

  2. Silent
    When Fathah/Kasrah/Dhammah is followed by Sukoon or Shaddah, don't read the letters in between.

  3. Madd Lazim Mutsaqal Harfi
    The recitation located at the beginning or opening segment of the Quranic verses. There are eight letters:the letter nun (ن), the letter qaf (ق), the letter shad (ص), the letter ain (ع), the letter sin (س), the letter lam (ل), the letter kaf (ك), and the letter mim (م).

  4. Madd Lazim Mukhofaf Harfi
    When the letter of prolongation (mad) meets a sukun within a letter. Linguistically, Mukhaffaf means light, while harfi means letter. Mad Lazim Mukhaffaf Harfi is one of the parts of Mad Far'i in the science of Tajweed.

  5. Madd Arid Lissukun
    Lengthening the recitation because the prolongation letter (mad) meets a letter with sukun due to stopping (waqaf) at the end of the verse.

  6. Madd Jaiz
    Natural prolongation (mad thabi'i) which meets with hamzah, but not within one word. The recitation is extended up to two and a half alifs or five vowel counts.

  7. Madd Wajib
    Natural prolongation (mad thabi'i) which meets with hamzah (ء) within one word. The length of obligatory continuous prolongation is four to five vowel counts or up to two and a half alifs.

  8. Idgham Mimi
    This rule applies when a silent mim (مْ) meets the letter mim (م). The pronunciation becomes one, as if there is an emphasis on the mim (م) accompanied by nasalization (ghunnah).

  9. Idgham Bighunnah
    The rule of Quranic recitation that involves nasalization (ghunnah) when a silent nun or tanwin meets one of the four letters of idgham bighunnah, namely waw (و), mim (م), nun (ن), and ya (ي).

  10. Idgham Bilaghunnah
    When reading a silent nun or tanwin, the sound of the nun combines and merges with the following letter without pronouncing ghunnah or nasalization. The rule of idgham bilaghunnah occurs when a silent nun or tanwin meets the letters lam (ل) and ra (ر).

  11. Idgham Mutamatsilain
    The meeting of two similar letters in characteristics and articulation points, such as when a "dal" meets another "dal," is mandatory to merge (idgham).

  12. Idgham Mutajanisain
    The meeting of two letters with different characteristics but the same articulation point, where the first letter is in a state of sukun (quiescence) and the second letter has a vowel.

  13. Ikhfa
    Ikhfa means to conceal. The rule of recitation of ikhfa haqiqi applies when the letter nun sukun or tanwin meets the letters of ikhfa, which are Ta', Tsa', Jim, Dal, Dzal, Zay, Sin, Syin, Sod, Dhod, Tha', Zha, Fa', Qof, Kaf.

  14. Ikhfa Syafawi
    The rule of mim sakinah applies when in a verse there is a mim sakina (مْ) meeting a ba' (ب), then the way to recite it is with a slight concealment and a little nasal sound.

  15. Ghunnah
    The humming sound that is nearly present in several recitation rules. However, the most perfect ghunnah is the ghunnah that occurs when the letters mim and nun are shaddah. As for the humming sound or ghunnah, it is also found in the recitation rules of idgham, ikhfa, and iqlab.

  16. Iqlab
    Iqlab is one of the aspects of the rules of nun sakinah and tanwin. Linguistically, Iqlab means to change, while in the terminology of the science of Tajweed, iqlab means to change the pronunciation of nun sakinah or tanwin to mim if the letter after it is the letter ba.

  17. Qalqalah
    The recitation of the letters of qalqalah with a sound as if ticking or bouncing. The letters of qalqalah are five: ba, jim, dal, ta, and qaf.
